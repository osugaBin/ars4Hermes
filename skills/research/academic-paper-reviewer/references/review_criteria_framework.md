# Review Criteria Framework — 7-Dimension Rubric

## Universal dimensions

| # | Dimension | Weight | Focus |
|---|-----------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Originality & Innovation | 15% | New ideas, methods, data, or perspectives |
| 2 | Methodological Rigor | 25% | Design quality, reproducibility, statistical correctness |
| 3 | Evidence Sufficiency | 20% | Claims backed by data/citations, counter-arguments addressed |
| 4 | Argument Coherence | 15% | Logical flow, internal consistency, transitions |
| 5 | Clarity & Structure | 10% | Writing quality, organization, figure/table clarity |
| 6 | Literature Coverage | 10% | Key works cited, currency, critical engagement |
| 7 | Significance & Impact | 5% | Contribution to field, practical relevance |

## Paper type-specific criteria

### Empirical research — additional focus
- Hypothesis clarity (testable, theory-consistent)
- Variable operational definitions
- Internal validity (confounders controlled)
- External validity (generalizability)
- Statistical reporting (effect sizes, CIs)
- Conclusion conservatism (not overclaiming)

### Theoretical paper — additional focus
- Conceptual definition precision
- Argument logic chain completeness
- Counterargument handling
- Theoretical novelty
- Testability (generates testable propositions)

### Literature review / meta-analysis — additional focus
- Search strategy (PRISMA compliance)
- Inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Bias risk assessment
- Heterogeneity handling
- Synthesis method quality
- Publication bias assessment

### Case study — additional focus
- Case selection justification
- Theoretical vs convenience sampling
- Triangulation (multiple data sources)
- Context description thickness
- Analysis transferability
- Researcher reflexivity

### Policy brief — additional focus
- Policy problem definition clarity
- Stakeholder analysis
- Policy option comparison
- Feasibility assessment
- Evidence quality for recommendations
- Unintended consequences considered

## Common review biases to avoid

| Bias | How to avoid |
|------|-------------|
| Hypercriticism | Affirm strengths first; distinguish major from minor |
| Confirmation bias | Deliberately seek the paper's merits |
| Preference projection | Evaluate "can this method answer the question?" not "what would I do?" |
| Paradigm bias | Use criteria matching the paper's paradigm |
| Prestige bias | Judge the paper, not the author/institution |
| Novelty bias | Value replication studies too |
| Length bias | Evaluate content density, not page count |
| Language discrimination | Distinguish "needs editing" from "poor research" |

## Constructive feedback principles

1. **Specific, not vague**: "Section 3 para 2 lacks control variables" > "methodology has problems"
2. **Problem + reason + suggestion**: Every criticism needs all three
3. **Distinguish required from suggested**: Mandatory vs optional
4. **Acknowledge uncertainty**: "I'm not sure X accounts for Y" > "the author ignored Y"
5. **Respect the author**: Time and effort were invested regardless of quality
