# Source Quality Hierarchy — Evidence Grading Framework

## Evidence Pyramid (7 Levels)

```
         /\
        / I \        Systematic Reviews / Meta-Analyses
       /──────\
      /  II    \     Randomized Controlled Trials
     /──────────\
    /   III      \   Controlled Studies (non-randomized)
   /──────────────\
  /    IV          \  Case-Control / Cohort Studies
 /──────────────────\
/     V              \  Systematic Reviews of Descriptive Studies
/──────────────────────\
/      VI                \  Single Descriptive / Qualitative Studies
/──────────────────────────\
/       VII                  \  Expert Opinion / Committee Reports
```

## Detailed Levels

| Level | Type | Weight | Examples |
|-------|------|--------|----------|
| I | Systematic Reviews / Meta-Analyses | Highest | Cochrane Library, Campbell Collaboration |
| II | Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) | Very High | Pre-registered RCTs with adequate sample size |
| III | Controlled Studies (non-randomized) | High | Difference-in-differences, propensity score matching |
| IV | Case-Control / Cohort Studies | Moderate-High | Longitudinal observational studies |
| V | Systematic Reviews of Descriptive Studies | Moderate | Meta-synthesis, meta-ethnography |
| VI | Single Descriptive / Qualitative Studies | Low-Moderate | Case studies, ethnographies, surveys |
| VII | Expert Opinion / Committee Reports | Lowest | Position papers, editorials, guidelines |

## Field-Specific Adjustments

Not all fields use the same evidence hierarchy:

| Field | Gold Standard | Common Level |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Medicine/Health | Level I-II (RCTs, meta-analyses) | Level I-III |
| Education | Level III-IV (quasi-experimental) | Level IV-VI |
| Social Science | Level III-V | Level IV-VI |
| Policy | Level IV-V + VII | Level V-VII |
| Humanities | Level VI (primary sources) | Level VI-VII |
| Technology | Level III + industry reports | Level V-VII |

## Simplified Grading Scale (for quick assessment)

| Grade | Meaning | Typical Levels |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| A | Primary evidence | I, II (or field gold standard) |
| B | Good supporting evidence | III, IV |
| C | Adequate with caveats | V, VI |
| D | Weak, use only if necessary | VII, grey literature |
| F | Do not use | Predatory, self-published, no methodology |

## Predatory Publication Red Flags

- Aggressive email solicitation to submit
- Acceptance within 72 hours
- No identifiable editorial board (or fake names)
- Not indexed in Scopus, Web of Science, or PubMed
- Not a COPE member
- Not listed in DOAJ
- Excessively broad scope ("International Journal of Everything")
- Fake or inflated impact metrics
- APC suspiciously low (< $200)
- No retraction policy or ethics guidelines
